Assignments

In addition to the lecture, you will build a web application (frontend and backend) in four group assignments (A1 to A4) in groups of 3 students:
  • Assignment 1 (A1) - HTML/CSS: You are given the design guide for the online shop "Artmart" and are tasked with producing a static frontend in HTML5 and CSS3 that is responsive and accessible.
  • Assignment 2 (A2) - JavaScript: You introduce interactivity to the existing static frontend with JavaScript by dynamically retrieving picture data from the Metropolitan Museum of Art Collection API and allowing users to configure picture frames on the fly by adapting the Document Object Model (DOM).
  • Assignment 3 (A3) - Node.js: You build part of the backend for Artmart as a web service in Node.js in JavaScript.
  • Assignment 4 (A4) - Vue.js: You introduce frontend abstractions for Artmart in Vue.js

Grading

Your grade will be a combination of the theoretical part (tests) and practical part (assignments). The theoretical and practical part affect the grade at a ratio of 1:1.

Theoretical part

  • Two written tests (50 points each)
    • Test 1: L1 – L5
    • Test 2: L6 – L10
    • At least 50% (25 points) on each test
  • The tests will be held online on TUWEL. You will need a stable internet connection. Feel free to join with your laptop to class at Informatikhörsaal.

Please beware that this is an experiment!
If over the course of the semester we see major obstacles with this policy, we will revert to paper tests in class.

Practical part

  • At least 51% overall (51 points of 100)
  • At least 30% of each mandatory assignment (8,5 points of 25)
